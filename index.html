Data Sources & Methodology
  • Primary Sources: Macroeconomic data (GDP, Growth, Exchange Rates) from MoSPI, RBI, and the World Bank. State-level FDI from DPIIT. Market Capitalization from NSE historical records, and Unemployment estimates from harmonized PLFS / CMIE / ILO data.
  • Data Interpolation: Missing historical reporting years in regional records are seamlessly filled using exponential mathematical interpolation. This inherently enforces the demographic accounting identity (`Per Capita = Nominal / Population`) across gap years.
  • Real GDP & Valuation: Real GDP is constructed via chain-linking to a 2022-23 base year to isolate true volume growth from inflation. To prevent currency volatility noise, Real USD metrics apply a constant base-year exchange rate.
  • Indicator Isolation: Individual states do not possess independent stock exchanges or distinct international USD reporting. Therefore, selecting USD Valuation or NSE Market Cap automatically disables state comparisons to isolate the National view for mathematical accuracy.
